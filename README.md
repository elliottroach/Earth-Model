# Seismic Waves

P-Waves:
    P-Waves are the fastest seismic wave and can travel through solids, liquids or gases. P-Waves move fastest through solids, slower in liquids and even slower in gases. P-Waves move like a train bumping into a cart and the cart bumping into another cart and so on.

S-Waves:
    S-Waves are slower than P-Waves and can only travel through solids. S-Waves move up and down like an ocean wave.

# Legend
For 200-200-200 scale

Inner Core: 
    The inner core is 1220 km in radius made of solid nickel and iron, at 5,430 °C. Depicted as the yellow/white in model images and is 38.3 mm thick.

Outer Core:
    The outer core is 2250 km thick and is made of liquid nickel and iron, at 2,700–4,200 °C. In the model it is not perfectly round because in earth it is not perfectly round. Depicted as the orange in model images and is 70.6 mm thick.

Mantle:
    The mantle is 2900 km thick and is made of molten rock called magma and is 230 °C. In the model it is not perfectly round because there are large misplaced chunks of rock. Depicted as the red in model images. The mantle is split into two parts one part of the asthenosphere the other part of the lithosphere and is 91.1 mm thick.

Oceanic Crust:
    The oceanic crust is 5 to 7 km thick and is made of solid rock. Depicted as the blue in model images and is 0.2 mm thick.

Continental Crust:
    The continental crust is 10 to 75 km thick and is made of solid rock. Depicted as the blue in model images and is 0.3 mm thick.

# Asthenosphere/Lithosphere Model
Lower Mantle:
    The lower mantle is 2900 km thick and is made of molten rock called magma. Depicted as the red in model images. The mantle is 69.06 mm thick in the model.

Transition Zone: 
    The Transition Zone is the change of minerals in earth's lower and upper mantle. Depicted as the change from red to pink.
    
Asthenosphere:
    The asthenosphere is roughly 200 km deep in the mantle to 700 km deep. Depicted as the pink in model images and is 15.7 mm thick

Lithosphere:
    The lithosphere consists of continental crust, oceanic crust and the upper mantle. It is from the surface to 200 km deep. Depicted as the brown in model images and is 6.28 mm thick
